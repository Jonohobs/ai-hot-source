## Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2) — All Free

### 1. Google Business Profile — The Single Most Important Thing

GBP drives the "local pack" (the map results that appear above organic results). For "plumber near me" or "plumber Glen Eden," this is where 70%+ of clicks go.

**Setup checklist:**
- **Primary category:** "Plumber"
- **Secondary categories:** "Drain cleaning service," "Plumbing contractor," "Water heater installation service"
- **Service area:** Set Glen Eden, West Auckland suburbs explicitly (Henderson, New Lynn, Titirangi, Green Bay, Avondale, Te Atatu, Massey, Kelston, Sunnyvale)
- **Business description:** Include "licensed plumber and drainlayer," "West Auckland," "7+ years experience," key services
- **Services list:** Add every service individually (blocked drains, hot water cylinder, leak detection, bathroom renovation, new builds, pipe repair, gas fitting if applicable, drain laying)
- **Attributes:** Enable messaging, set business hours, add payment methods
- **Photos:** Minimum 10 to start. Van, tools, before/after work, Steve in uniform/at work. Add 1-2 new photos per week.

**GBP posting strategy (free, 10 min/week):**
- Post 1x per week minimum. Google rewards active profiles.
- Rotate: job completion photo, seasonal tip, service highlight, offer/promotion
- Posts expire after 7 days from search visibility — consistency matters

**What drives local pack ranking (in order of importance):**
1. **Proximity** to searcher (can't control, but service area setting helps)
2. **GBP completeness & activity** (photos, posts, Q&A, updates)
3. **Review quantity, quality & recency** (see below)
4. **Category relevance** (primary + secondary categories)
5. **Website signals** (NAP consistency, local content)
6. **Citation consistency** across directories

### 2. Review Generation — The Growth Engine

**The data is clear:**
- 97% of consumers read reviews before choosing a local business
- 47% won't use a business with fewer than 20 reviews
- 74% prioritize reviews from the last 3 months
- 68% require minimum 4-star rating
- 80% more likely to use businesses that respond to ALL reviews

**Steve's review generation system (free):**

**Step 1: Create a short review link**
- In GBP dashboard, go to "Ask for reviews" — copy the short link
- Save it in phone contacts as "Review Link"

**Step 2: The ask (timing is everything)**
- Ask IN PERSON at job completion when the customer is happy: *"Really glad we sorted that for you. If you've got 30 seconds, a Google review would mean the world — I'll text you the link now."*
- Send the link via text WITHIN 10 MINUTES of leaving the job
- Template SMS: *"Thanks for choosing Poseidon Plumbing, [Name]! If you're happy with the work, a quick Google review helps us massively: [link] — Cheers, Steve"*

**Step 3: Follow-up (if no review in 3 days)**
- One gentle follow-up only: *"Hey [Name], just following up — if you get a sec to leave a review that'd be awesome: [link]. No worries if not! — Steve"*

**Step 4: Respond to EVERY review**
- Same day if possible, within 48 hours max
- Personalized responses, NOT templates. Mention the specific job.
- Negative reviews: acknowledge, thank, offer to make it right offline. Never argue.

**Target:** 2-3 reviews per week. Within 3 months Steve will have 25-35 reviews, which puts him ahead of most West Auckland plumbers.

**Automation tools (for later):**
- **Jobber** ($35 USD/mo) — job management + automatic review request emails after job completion
- **NiceJob** ($75 USD/mo) — dedicated review automation, SMS + email sequences
- **Tradify** (NZ-made, ~$35 NZD/mo) — job management built for NZ tradies, has review request features
- For now: just use phone + saved text templates. Don't spend money until revenue justifies it.

## Phase 2: Content & Community (Week 2-4)

### 4. Facebook Community Group Strategy — Free Leads

This is one of the highest-ROI activities for a local tradesperson. NZ community Facebook groups are extremely active.

**Target groups to join:**
- Glen Eden Community
- West Auckland Community Group
- Henderson Community
- New Lynn Community
- Titirangi & Surrounds
- Green Bay Community
- Te Atatu Community
- Auckland Home Improvement / Renovations
- "Recommend a Tradesperson Auckland"
- "West Auckland Buy Sell Trade"

**The strategy:**
1. **Answer questions helpfully** — when someone asks "does anyone know a plumber" or "my tap is dripping, any advice?" — answer with genuine advice FIRST, then mention availability
2. **Don't spam** — 80% helpful advice, 20% "happy to take a look if you need a hand"
3. **Share job photos** — "Just finished this bathroom reno in Glen Eden" with before/after photos
4. **Be the local expert** — post seasonal tips ("Winter's coming Auckland — here's how to check your hot water cylinder before it dies on you")
5. **Response time matters** — people asking for a plumber in community groups often need one TODAY. Be fast.

**NZ equivalent to Nextdoor:**
- **Neighbourly.co.nz** — NZ's local community platform (similar concept to Nextdoor). Worth claiming a business presence if available in West Auckland. Check and claim.

### 5. Before/After Photo Habit

**Rule:** Photograph EVERY job. Before, during (optional), after. This feeds everything else.

**How to do it efficiently:**
- Quick phone photo before starting. One after completion.
- Get verbal permission from customer: "Mind if I take a photo for my portfolio?"
- Store in a dedicated phone album: "Poseidon Jobs"
- Best ones go to: GBP posts, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok

**What photographs well:**
- Drain unblocking (the satisfying reveal)
- Bathroom renovations (dramatic before/after)
- Hot water cylinder replacements (old rusty vs new clean)
- Leak repairs (water damage before, fixed after)
- Outdoor drainage work (trenches, pipe laying)

### 6. Educational Content Ideas (for social + GBP posts)

**Seasonal content for NZ:**

| Season | Content |
|--------|---------|
| **Autumn (Mar-May)** | "Check your gutters before winter," "Is your hot water cylinder over 15 years old? Here's why that matters" |
| **Winter (Jun-Aug)** | "Frozen pipe prevention for Auckland homes," "Why your shower goes cold (and how to fix it)," "Emergency plumber — what to do before I arrive" |
| **Spring (Sep-Nov)** | "Spring plumbing check: 5 things to look at," "Planning a bathroom reno? Start now for summer" |
| **Summer (Dec-Feb)** | "Outdoor shower installation," "Holiday plumbing checklist before you leave," "Garden irrigation basics" |

**Evergreen content (works year-round):**
- "5 signs your hot water cylinder needs replacing"
- "What to do in a plumbing emergency (before the plumber arrives)"
- "Why you should never pour grease down the drain"
- "Licensed vs unlicensed plumber — why it matters for your insurance"
- "What does a drainlayer actually do?" (Steve's dual qualification is a selling point)
- "How much does a plumber cost in Auckland? Honest pricing guide"
- "The #1 thing homeowners do that destroys their pipes"

## Phase 3: Physical Marketing & Video (Week 3-8)

### 7. Vehicle Branding

One of the highest-ROI physical marketing investments. The van is a moving billboard in West Auckland every day.

**Options (NZ pricing estimates):**
| Type | Cost | Impact |
|------|------|--------|
| Vinyl lettering (name, phone, logo) | $300-600 | Good |
| Partial wrap (sides + rear) | $800-1,500 | Better |
| Full wrap | $2,000-4,000 | Best, but overkill for bootstrapping |

**What to include:**
- Business name: POSEIDON PLUMBING (large, readable from 20m)
- Phone number (large, readable)
- "Licensed Plumber & Drainlayer"
- Service area: "West Auckland"
- Website/social if applicable
- Clean, professional design. Trident/water theme fits the brand.

**Recommendation:** Start with vinyl lettering ($300-600). Upgrade to partial wrap when revenue supports it.

**Where to get it in Auckland:**
- Search "vehicle signwriting Auckland" — get 3 quotes
- Ask in tradie Facebook groups for recommendations

**Pro tip:** Park the branded van on the street (not in the garage) at home and at every job. Free advertising 24/7.

### 8. Short-Form Video Strategy

Plumber content does extremely well on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. The format rewards authenticity over production quality.

**What works for plumber accounts (proven viral formats):**
1. **Satisfying reveals** — drain unblocking, pressure testing, water flowing clean
2. **"What I found behind this wall"** — surprise/horror reveals
3. **Day in the life** — time-lapse of a full job, Auckland scenery
4. **Quick tips** — 30-second "don't do this" or "do this instead"
5. **Before/after transformations** — bathrooms especially
6. **Responding to comments** — builds engagement
7. **"How much does a plumber charge for..."** — addressing price questions builds trust
8. **Kiwi personality** — lean into the NZ accent, humor, "she'll be right" culture

**Successful plumber accounts to study:**
- @theplumber (TikTok) — millions of views on drain unblocking content
- @plumberparts (YouTube, 1M+ subs) — educational + personality
- @rogertheplumber (TikTok) — day-in-the-life format
- Search "plumber" on TikTok and study what gets views in your niche

**Getting started (free):**
- Film on phone, no fancy equipment needed
- 1-2 videos per week is plenty
- Use trending audio on TikTok/Reels
- Hashtags: #plumber #plumbinglife #auckland #westtaukland #plumbingtips #tradielife #nztradies
- Cross-post the same video to TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, and Facebook

**The kicker:** Even if a video doesn't go viral, it builds a portfolio of credibility. When a potential customer Googles "Poseidon Plumbing" and finds video content, trust goes through the roof.

## Phase 4: Growth Acceleration (Month 2+)

### 9. Simple Website + Local SEO

Not urgent if GBP is working, but valuable for credibility and organic search.

**Cheap options:**
- **Google Sites** (free, built into GBP)
- **Carrd.co** ($19/year) — beautiful single-page sites
- **Square Online** (free tier)
- **WordPress.com** (free tier)

**Must-have pages:**
- Home (services, service area, phone number, "Licensed Plumber & Drainlayer")
- Services (one section per service, mention Auckland/West Auckland suburbs)
- About (Steve's story, qualifications, 7+ years)
- Contact (phone, email, service area map)
- Reviews/testimonials

**Local SEO basics:**
- Title tag: "Poseidon Plumbing | Licensed Plumber & Drainlayer | West Auckland"
- Include suburb names naturally in content
- Embed Google Map
- Link to/from all directory listings

### 10. Referral Program

Word of mouth is the #1 lead source for NZ tradies. Formalize it.

**Simple referral card system:**
- Print 200 business cards with referral offer on the back
- Offer: "$50 off your next job when you refer a friend" (or similar)
- Leave 2-3 cards with every happy customer
- *"If you know anyone who needs a plumber, I'd appreciate the referral — there's a discount for both of you on the back of the card."*

**Digital version:**
- Same offer via text: *"Know someone who needs a plumber? If they mention your name, you both get $50 off. — Steve, Poseidon Plumbing"*

**Cost:** ~$30-50 for business cards. The $50 discount comes out of margin but the customer acquisition cost is far below Google Ads.

### 11. Google Ads (Phase 4 — Only When Ready)

**Only consider Google Ads when:**
- GBP is fully optimized with 20+ reviews
- Website exists and converts
- You can handle the extra lead volume

**NZ/Auckland cost benchmarks (approximate):**
- **Cost per click:** $8-25 NZD for plumbing keywords in Auckland (competitive market)
- **Cost per lead:** $40-120 NZD via Google Ads
- **Conversion rate:** ~5-15% from click to call/form submission
- **Minimum viable budget:** $500-1,000 NZD/month to test

**Compare to organic:**
- Organic lead via GBP/SEO: effectively $0 per lead
- Referral lead: ~$50 per lead (discount cost)
- Facebook community lead: $0 per lead
- This is why organic comes first.

**If/when Steve does Google Ads:**
- Start with **Google Local Services Ads** (pay-per-lead, not per-click, and shows the green "Google Guaranteed" badge)
- Target: "emergency plumber West Auckland," "plumber Glen Eden," "blocked drain Auckland"
- Set a daily budget cap ($20-30/day to start)
- Track every lead source to measure ROI

## What NOT to Spend Money On (Yet)

1. **SEO agencies** — most are overpriced and underdeliver for trades. Do the basics yourself first.
2. **Paid Facebook ads** — organic community engagement outperforms paid ads for local trades.
3. **Fancy website** — a simple one-page site is enough until revenue justifies more.
4. **Print advertising** (newspapers, flyers) — very low ROI in 2026.
5. **Lead generation companies** that charge per lead — build your own pipeline first.
6. **Instagram/TikTok paid promotion** — organic reach is still strong for trades content.
